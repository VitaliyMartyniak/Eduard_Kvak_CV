Car IQ Pay

Car IQ Pay is the first payment platform to enable vehicles to initiate, validate, and complete authorized transactions. The system eliminates the credit card completely and connects the vehicle directly to merchants to make secure and seamless payments. Car IQ Pay integrates with existing tech stacks and uses telematics and vehicle data to provide fleets with detailed insights, allowing them to follow every payment and eliminate fraud. As a result, it consolidates all transactions into a single ledger, reducing reconciliation and enabling fleets to manage their spending more effectively.

Overview

The goal was to develop a mobile app for drivers and a web app for fleet managers. These apps needed to provide unique functionalities and a seamless user experience to streamline vehicle-initiated transactions and fleet management.

Responsibilities

Conducting user research to understand user needs, behaviors, and preferences.
Engaging in user interviews for feedback and design validation.
Creating scalable product architecture.
Developing designs, documentation, specifications, and guidelines.
Creating white-label solutions, Apple CarPlay/Android Auto integrations, and other extensions.
Collaborating with developers on task creation, design QA, etc.
Iteratively improving product usability and functionality through user data and feedback.
Creating prototypes and product assets for successful development.

Challenges

Limited user experience with smartphones and alternative payment methods like Apple Pay or Google Pay.
Constraints from developers, databases, data security protocols, and major companies (Apple and Google).
Visual design principles that can easily adapt to the other companies' branding.
Meeting AA-level accessibility requirements.
Multiple language support.
Complex fleet structure hierarchy and multiple access levels.

Working process

Explore the various stages below to gain insight into our approach and methodologies.

We usually start each new sprint by conducting thorough analysis of the data and feedback gathered from the previous sprint. Next, we facilitate a brainstorming session to ideate and define new features and enhancements that contribute to product objectives. Following the brainstorming session, we proceed to create and assign tasks based on the prioritization of features. After receiving the tasks, my first step is to create wireframes to visualize and validate new features. This iterative process involves multiple rounds of refinement to ensure optimal usability and functionality. Once the wireframes are finalized and approved, I proceed to develop the final designs and components. This includes creating high-fidelity mockups, prototypes, etc. Additionally, I prepare all necessary design documentation and guidelines for developers. To accommodate potential iterations and unforeseen challenges, we allocate approximately half of the sprint for the design phase. This allows for additional time for analysis or adjustments as needed. Finally, I conduct a collaborative working session with developers to ensure alignment and clarity on the implementation of design elements. This includes sharing design assets, specifications, and any additional context or requirements for the current sprint. Once developers receive all necessary design assets and documentation, they commence their work on implementing the features and functionalities outlined in the sprint tasks. Throughout the development process, developers collaborate closely with me to address any issues or changes that may arise. This allows for timely adjustments to be made to the design as needed, ensuring that the final product meets the intended requirements and objectives. After developers complete their work, they deploy a new build to the mock environment. I then proceed to conduct thorough testing of the new build to identify any inconsistencies or issues. During testing, I focus not only on functional aspects but also on aspects related to design integrity, such as animations and interaction with components. This comprehensive approach helps to uncover any potential weaknesses in the design. If any discrepancies or bugs are discovered during testing, I create detailed bug tickets outlining the issue and its impact. These tickets are then addressed by developers to ensure timely resolution and maintain the quality of the final product. After addressing all identified bugs and ensuring the stability of the application, developers proceed to release a new build in the production environment, making it available to users. To gather insights and monitor user interactions post-launch, we leverage tools such as Pendo to create new anchors and track potential pain points in the user journey. Additionally, we conduct A/B testing to validate hypotheses and optimize the user experience based on data-driven insights. In some cases, we supplement quantitative data with qualitative feedback by conducting user interviews or usability testing sessions. This allows us to gain deeper insights into user behavior and preferences, informing future iterations and improvements.
